LETTERING

The creation of this lettering required a wide research over a lot of different styles, ranging from complex and articulated to simple and linear ones. After numerous sketches that aimed to mostly employ smooth lines and shapes, I came to the realisation that focusing on more geometrical compositions came both easier and natural to me. During the early stages of planning the design got through many corrections and changes, since instead of constructing every letter from the same pool of shapes I opted to create them following a broad set of rules aimed to make them as cohesive as possible. The more common themes are the multiple "rays" that start from the same point used for the letters wich are mostly comprised by straight lines, and the combination of said rays with circumferences for letters that presented one or more curves. This specific lettering was not created for a specific usage, but given its characteristics and the fact that it becomes very hard to read when shrunk it is best suited for titles or similar tasks that require a larger font size to maximise its impact on the reader.

LOGO

This project focuses around the theme of esotericism, a term that refers to sets of beliefs that are by their own nature "closed off" to the great majority of the population. This specific project was made for an hypotetical esoteric shop, a place that sells items related to various cults including, but not limited to, paganism, hinduism, wicca and buddhism. Given the great amount of cults and their vast usage of iconography, the hardest part of the project was choosing a very limited number of symbols to work with; for this purpose, the Baphomet and the eye were chosen given their widespread usage and recognizability even from people with no interest in the subject. The specific colors chosen are black, purple, gold and silver, both for their meaning and because of the chromatic combination.

  • Black for purification.
  • Purple for protection and sensitivity.
  • Gold to attract fortune.
  • Silver for meditation.
